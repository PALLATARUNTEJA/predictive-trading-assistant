Market Terms Dictionary

Market Trends

Bullish
A market state where prices are rising or expected to rise. Buyers are in control, creating positive momentum. Our dashboard highlights Bullish indicators when stock close is above both SMA 20 and SMA 50.
Bearish
A market state where prices are falling or expected to fall. Sellers dominate, leading to price declines. If a stock falls below its moving averages, it enters a bearish condition.

Risk Management & Safety

Stop-Loss (Auto-Exit)
A protective risk management rule. If you buy a stock at $100 and set a 20% Stop-Loss (exiting when value becomes less than 80), the system monitors prices in real time and automatically triggers a sell order instantly to protect you from heavy losses.
Daily Drawdown Limit
An institutional-grade risk filter. If your entire portfolio loses more than your set slider threshold (e.g. 5%) of its starting cash value today, trading is immediately frozen to prevent further losses.

Technical Indicators

RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A momentum oscillator ranging from 0 to 100. Values above 70 (Overbought) suggest the stock is over-extended and might reverse down. Values below 30 (Oversold) indicate the stock is undervalued and might bounce upward.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
A trend-following momentum indicator. When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line (Bullish crossover), it signals a buying opportunity. When it crosses below (Bearish crossover), it implies downward momentum.
